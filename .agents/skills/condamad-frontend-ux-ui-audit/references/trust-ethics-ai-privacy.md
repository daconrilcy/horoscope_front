# Trust, Ethics, AI, Privacy, and Safety Rules

Use these rules when auditing pricing, checkout, consent, subscription,
cancellation, AI features, personalization, sensitive actions, security, and
privacy.

## AI in the Experience

- AI must solve a real user friction: reduce time, effort, uncertainty, or
  improve decision quality.
- The user must understand whether AI recommends, summarizes, generates,
  ranks, automates, or decides.
- The user must be able to correct, regenerate, undo, compare, restore, or
  return to manual mode.
- Sensitive AI-influenced decisions must be explainable through criteria,
  sources, or signals.

Block readiness when:

- AI publishes, deletes, sends, buys, classifies, or materially changes
  something important without human validation;
- AI is opaque on a sensitive decision;
- AI output is irreversible without confirmation.

## Ethics and Anti-Dark-Patterns

- Refusing must be as easy as accepting for consent, subscriptions, marketing,
  and optional tracking.
- Costs, fees, conditions, commitment, renewal, and cancellation must be shown
  before final action.
- Cancellation, deletion, or unsubscribe paths must be findable and completable
  without artificial obstacles.
- Urgency or scarcity must be real, verifiable, and relevant.

Block readiness when:

- accept is prominent and refuse is hidden or extremely de-emphasized;
- fees appear only at the final step;
- cancellation is deliberately long, hidden, or guilt-driven;
- timers, stock, or urgency claims are artificial.

## Trust, Security, and Privacy

- Sensitive actions require confirmation: deletion, payment, transfer,
  publication, sending, irreversible modification.
- Data collection must be justified when the data is sensitive, unusual, or not
  obviously needed.
- Privacy settings must be findable from account, preferences, or footer.
- Security messages must explain the issue, risk level, and next step without
  panic or technical opacity.

Block readiness when:

- an irreversible action happens in one click;
- personal or sensitive data is requested without context;
- privacy settings are hidden;
- security copy is alarming or incomprehensible without a next action.

## Personalization

- Personalization must be explainable: history, preference, location, segment,
  or recent behavior.
- Personalization must be editable, resettable, or disableable.
- Personalization must simplify decisions, not hide options or bias users into
  less favorable choices.

Block readiness when:

- important options are hidden because they are not "optimized";
- recommendations are opaque and consequential;
- personalization removes user control.

## Audit Questions

- Does the user know the cost, commitment, and consequence before acting?
- Is refusing as easy as accepting?
- Can the user cancel or reverse important decisions?
- Does the interface explain why data is requested?
- Does AI act with clear user control?
- Are personalized recommendations explainable and controllable?
